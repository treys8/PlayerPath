EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
==================================================================

No CRITICAL or HIGH bugs. Several MEDIUM findings, most centered
around cross-account leak on sign-out (local notifications keep
firing for the previous account) and one trigger-type mismatch
(UNCalendarNotificationTrigger used where UNTimeIntervalNotification
would be correct).

GameAlertService is clean. PushNotificationService has the most
findings.

MEDIUM FINDINGS
==================================================================

PushNotificationService.swift:357-358 — MEDIUM (wrong trigger type
for "starts soon" reminder)
  Quote:
    `let trigger = UNCalendarNotificationTrigger(
            dateMatching: Calendar.current.dateComponents([.year, .month, .day, .hour, .minute], from: reminderDate),`
  Why: scheduleGameReminder builds a calendar trigger from
  year/month/day/hour/minute. UNCalendarNotificationTrigger fires
  when components match the user's CURRENT calendar/timezone, not
  the absolute Date represented by reminderDate. Timezone change
  between scheduling and fire (travel, DST jump) or a non-Gregorian
  Calendar.current shifts the wall-clock fire moment off the
  intended absolute time. For a 30-minute "starts soon" reminder
  this is the wrong trigger type.
  Fix: use UNTimeIntervalNotificationTrigger(timeInterval:
  reminderDate.timeIntervalSinceNow, repeats: false) — the function
  already guards reminderDate > Date() above. Or pass
  Calendar(identifier: .gregorian) explicitly.

PushNotificationService.swift:394-401 — MEDIUM (no cancel on sign-out
of repeating coach reminder)
  Quote:
    `var dateComponents = DateComponents()
        dateComponents.hour = hour
        dateComponents.minute = minute

        let trigger = UNCalendarNotificationTrigger(
            dateMatching: dateComponents,
            repeats: true
        )`
  Why: identifier `Self.reviewReminderID = "coach_review_reminder"`
  with repeats: true. cancelReviewReminder() exists but is NOT
  invoked from any sign-out flow. When the user signs out, the
  repeating reminder continues to fire on a device that may now be
  signed into a different account, leaking content across accounts.
  Fix: call PushNotificationService.shared.cancelReviewReminder()
  (and cancel all athlete weekly_summary_* identifiers) from the
  sign-out routine in ComprehensiveAuthManager.

PushNotificationService.swift:443 — MEDIUM (weekly summary leaks
to next account after sign-out)
  Quote:
    `cancelNotifications(withIdentifiers: ["weekly_summary_\(athleteId)"])`
  Why: weekly summary notifications keyed per athlete UUID. On
  sign-out there's no enumeration of past athleteIds. Prior
  athletes' weekly summaries remain pending; they deliver "Your
  Week in Review" content for athletes no longer signed-in, leaking
  the previous account's athleteId in userInfo. repeats: false so
  it self-clears after one fire, but the window between sign-out
  and the next Sunday 6 PM is the leak window.
  Fix: on sign-out, call
  UNUserNotificationCenter.current().removeAllPendingNotificationRequests()
  OR filter pending requests by the "weekly_summary_" prefix and
  remove them.

PushNotificationService.swift:463 — MEDIUM (batting average format
breaks at 1.000)
  Quote:
    `let avgFormatted = String(format: ".%03.0f", avg * 1000)`
  Why: for avg == 1.0, this prints ".1000" (four digits) instead of
  "1.000". For avg > 1.0 it prints garbage like ".1500". Cosmetic
  correctness, not a crash.
  Fix: use StatisticsService.shared.formatBattingAverage(avg) which
  already exists and is used by PDFReportGenerator/CSVExportService.

WeeklySummaryScheduler.swift:23-28 — MEDIUM (locale-dependent
week boundary)
  Quote:
    `var fireComponents = DateComponents()
        fireComponents.weekday = 1 // Sunday
        fireComponents.hour = 18
        fireComponents.minute = 0
        guard let fireDate = calendar.nextDate(after: Date(), matching: fireComponents, matchingPolicy: .nextTime),
              let windowStart = calendar.date(byAdding: .day, value: -7, to: fireDate) else {`
  Why: Calendar.current may be Persian, Hebrew, Buddhist, or
  Islamic. DateComponents.weekday == Sunday holds across iOS built-in
  calendars where the standard mapping applies (so no off-by-one
  defect today). BUT calendar.firstWeekday is locale-derived; the
  -7-day window may not align with the user's actual locale week.
  Not a crash; behavior is correct for US users.
  Fix (optional): use Calendar(identifier: .gregorian) explicitly to
  remove locale variability.
