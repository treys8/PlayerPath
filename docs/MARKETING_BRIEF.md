# PlayerPath — Marketing Brief

*Prepared for external marketing/creative partners. Last updated 2026-06-03.*

---

## 1. What PlayerPath Is

**PlayerPath is an iOS app that helps youth and amateur athletes record their games, track their stats, and grow with the help of a coach — all in one place.**

It's built for **baseball, softball, and golf** athletes (more sports planned), and for the **private/skills coaches** who work with them. Think of it as a personal performance journal that doubles as a coaching tool: every video, stat, photo, and lesson lives in one timeline that follows the athlete across seasons and years.

### The core loop
1. **Record** — Athletes film at-bats, pitches, swings, or full rounds right in the app, then tag each play (single, strikeout, birdie, etc.).
2. **Track** — The app turns those tagged plays into real statistics — batting average, ERA, scoring relative to par — across games, seasons, and a full career.
3. **Review & coach** — Athletes share clips with a coach, who marks them up with on-screen drawing (telestration), drill cards, and notes, then sends feedback back.
4. **Keep** — Everything is saved as a personal "journal" the athlete builds over time — videos, photos, milestones — a keepsake of their athletic story.

### Who it's for
- **Primary:** Youth & high-school athletes (and their parents, who often manage the account) in baseball, softball, and golf.
- **Secondary:** Private hitting/pitching/skills coaches and small training academies who give one-on-one instruction.
- **Note on coaching:** Coach sharing is built for **personal instruction (private lessons)**, not team practice management. That's a deliberate positioning choice.

### What makes it different
- **One home for the whole athletic journey** — video + stats + photos + coaching, not four separate apps.
- **Real, automatic stats** — tagging a play once feeds a full statistics engine; no manual spreadsheet work.
- **Built-in private coaching** — telestration, drill cards, and clip feedback are native, not bolted on.
- **Multi-sport, multi-athlete** — one account (e.g. a parent) can host multiple athlete profiles across different sports.
- **A keepsake, not just a tool** — the journal framing makes the app something families want to keep, which drives long-term retention.

## 2. The New Look — "Calm Keepsake" Visual Overhaul

The app was just redesigned around a new identity we call **"Calm Keepsake."** This is the single most important thing for any new creative work to match. The old look was a typical sporty navy-and-gold, high-contrast tech app. The new look is **warm, editorial, and personal** — it feels like a premium printed keepsake or a well-made journal, not a stats dashboard.

**Design principles to carry into all marketing:**
- **Warm & calm, not loud.** Cream surfaces, soft earth tones, generous whitespace.
- **Editorial & personal.** It should feel like a keepsake / scrapbook / fine print product, not a hype sports brand.
- **Light only.** The app is intentionally locked to a light appearance — the cream background *is* the brand. Marketing should lean into light, airy layouts.
- **One accent at a time.** A single accent color signals importance/action. It is restrained on purpose; don't introduce competing bright colors.

### Typography
The app pairs a **serif display face** with a **clean humanist sans** — a key part of the "editorial keepsake" feel. Marketing should mirror this pairing.

- **Display & headings:** **Fraunces** (a warm, high-contrast serif) — Bold for big display, SemiBold for headings.
- **Body, labels, UI:** **Inter** (Regular / Medium / SemiBold) — clean, modern, highly legible.

> Pairing recommendation for collateral: **Fraunces** for headlines and emotional/story copy, **Inter** for body, captions, and any data/numbers.

### One-line creative direction
> *"A warm, editorial keepsake for an athlete's journey — cream paper, a single terracotta accent, a serif headline. Calm and personal, never loud."*

## 3. Features Worth Highlighting in Campaigns

**For athletes / parents**
- Record and tag game video in-app (or import from the camera roll).
- Automatic batting / pitching / golf statistics across games, seasons, and full career.
- Stat charts and season-over-season comparison.
- A personal **journal feed** — every game, practice, clip, and photo in one timeline.
- Photos as first-class memories, taggable to specific games/events.
- Multi-athlete support (great for families with more than one player).
- Multi-sport: baseball, softball, and golf today.

**For coaches**
- Receive athlete clips and give feedback with **on-screen telestration** (draw with finger or Apple Pencil).
- **Drill cards** and lesson notes attached to clips.
- Live instruction sessions that record clips straight into the athlete's "Needs Review" queue.
- Manage multiple athletes from a coach dashboard.

**Platform**
- iOS 17+ (iPhone and iPad), built natively in SwiftUI.
- Cloud sync so an athlete's data and clips are safe and available across devices.

## 5. Messaging Angles & Suggested Hooks

- **"Your whole season, in one place."** — the all-in-one journal angle.
- **"Tag the play. Get the stats."** — effortless, automatic statistics.
- **"Coaching that travels with you."** — private-lesson feedback, telestration, drills.
- **"A keepsake of every swing, pitch, and round."** — the emotional/retention angle (strong for parents).
- **"One app for baseball, softball, and golf."** — multi-sport reach.

**Audience-specific:**
- *Parents:* peace of mind, watching their kid improve, a lasting record of their athletic years.
- *Athletes:* see real progress, share with coaches, build a highlight reel / future recruiting story.
- *Coaches:* deliver better feedback faster, manage your roster, look professional with telestration + drill cards.

## 6. Notes & Constraints for Creative Teams

- **Stay light & warm.** Do not produce dark-mode mockups of the app — it is light-only by design.
- **Respect the single-accent rule.** Terracotta `#C8693E` is the hero. Avoid loud orange/red CTAs that compete with it.
- **Golf gets green, everything else gets terracotta** — keep the two accents in their own contexts; don't mix them in one frame.
- **Tone:** warm, encouraging, family-friendly, premium-but-approachable. Not hype, not "grind culture."
- **Sports represented today:** baseball, softball, golf. Don't depict sports the app doesn't support yet.
- **Coaching = private instruction**, not team management — keep that distinction in any coach-facing copy.
